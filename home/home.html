In 2008, an anonymous person named “Satoshi Nakamoto” published the Bitcoin whitepaper online. The book is about Peer-to-peer electronic cash system (decentralized digital currency)

Today's transaction is all performed by a central authority (usually a bank), but bitcoin is decentralized, which means that anyone could perform someone else’s transaction.

Every time a transaction is performed, the information will be added to all the previous transaction records, which people called it a “Blockchain”.

The blockchain is encrypted and sent to every peer that’s processing transactions. The people that are performing transactions are called “Bitcoin Miners”.

Nakamoto also suggests a rewarding system to motivate people to perform transactions, if a transaction is happening, the sender has to pay extra bitcoin as transaction fee to the miner for performing their transaction. The miner who successfully performs the transaction, the system will reward the person extra Bitcoins.

When the Bitcoin system was established, the system was rewarded 50 Bitcoins per transaction, every 4 years the reward reduced by half. (This is 2019 and currently rewarding is 12.50)

There are only 21 millions Bitcoins in the system.
